EVENT DESCRIPTION

Robowar is an event where in manually controlled Robots battle each other. A robot is declared victorious if its opponent is immobilized, i.e. a robot will be declared immobilized if it cannot display any movement for a time period of 20 seconds. In case both the robots remain mobile after the end of the round then the winner will be decided subjectively based on the score calculated.
 
Base score is calculated based on three parameters:
Aggression, Control and Damage Extra points will be awarded
 
Based on using the arena and its specification constraints. For example, pushing the opponent’s bot into the red zone in an arena.
 
Build a robot whose task is to push the opposing robot out of the arena or flip it over. Different robots compete one-on-one against each other in a knock-out tournament

ROBOT SPECIFICATION

Size : At the start of a match the robot must fit into a square box of side 50 cm, with no height limitation. The design to stretch a robot's body or its parts shall be allowed after a match has started but must remain a single centralized robot.
Weight : The robot must weigh less than 30 KG (excluding the controls/wires) or less.
Robot Classes : Remote Controlled or Manual (Wired Control).
Power Specifications : The robot must be self-powered, i.e., power supply must be on-board. AC power supply will not be provided, except for charging your batteries (in the breaks only). No limit for power supply.  
 

ROUNDS

Round 1 : Four teams will start the game two opposite teams will fight with each other for two-minute center dump will be opened if opponent is fallen out of arena or flip the robot of opponent team then those teams will be qualified for further rounds and remaining teams may be given a second change as a wildcard entry in second round.
Round 2 : Two teams have to fight with each other according to number of attacks by each team points will be allocated. And any team will make opponent team inactive more than 20 second then that team will earn extra bonus points. Points rules will be declared at the time of the event.
Round 3 : Will be announce on spot.  
 

RULES AND REGULATIONS

  1. Each team can have maximum of 4 members. Students from different institutes are not allowed to form a team.
  2. Each member of the team must have the identity card of their respective institute.
  3. The robot should not damage the arena
  4. Unethical behavior will lead to disqualification. Faculty coordinators have all the rights to take final decision for any matter during the event.
  5. Judge’s decision will be considered final.
  6. Plane rough surface (tarred ground).
  7. There will be a fixed boundary, team crosses the boundary will be out.
  8. Inner circle or rounds are there inside the arena, point will be given to the team which move the opponent inside it, and negative points to the team inside the circle.
  9. Fixed obstacles are there.
  10. These rules are tentative and may change, if required.
  11. Your robot must be ready when call is made for your team.
  12. Machine must not contain any readymade kits, pneumatic and hydraulic systems, IC engines.
  13. Weapons are restricted to flippers only.
